OurSpace

Case Study

UX/UI Design Fernanda, Gustavo, Kristina

Space for Effective Communication

OurSpace is an app that helps couples manage conflicts more easily, promote mutual empathy, and strengthen their emotional bond sustainably.

Overview

Context

As part of our capstone project for the UX/UI Bootcamp, we designed and developed an app in a team of three. The concept is based on extensive research. Currently, the app consists of an initial clickable high-fidelity prototype.

Problem Statement

Many couples gradually forget how to communicate effectively over time or due to certain circumstances. Partners feel unheard or unseen in their relationship but do not know how to express this.

Goal

We aim to provide support for more effective communication in partnerships.

Benefits
Easier management of conflict situations
Establishing empathetic interactions
Strengthening emotional bonds

Research

“Respectful communication leads to healthier and more fulfilling relationships by creating space for genuine connection and mutual appreciation.”

Beth Luwandi Lofstrom

“69% of all relationship conflicts are ongoing; conflicts must be managed, not solved.”

Julie and John Gottman

Secondary Research

For our secondary research, we examined the findings of relationship researchers Dr. John and Dr. Julie Gottman, as well as Beth Luwandi Lofstrom. They studied the impact of different communication styles on relationships.

We found that conventional communication often aims to assert one’s own perspective and demand solutions, leading to power imbalances and defensive behaviors, as well as destructive actions.

To resolve conflicts constructively, it requires:
effective communication during disputes
expressing one’s own emotions
clarifying desires or boundaries
understanding the feelings of the other

Research shows that the quality of interactions during conflicts, rather than the absence of conflicts, is actually crucial for the health and longevity of a relationship.

Quantitative & Qualitative Research

We conducted a survey that ran for four days. 64 participants from Germany, Mexico, Colombia, Spain, India, and Uruguay took part. The majority of participants are or were recently in a long-term relationship. We also conducted four interviews.

The goal was to find out what issues exist in relationships, how partners feel about them, how they cope, and whether there is a need for assistance in improving communication difficulties.

These feelings are experienced by people after a relationship argument:

These hypotheses were supported by our research:

The issues of communication and lack of time are the most frequently mentioned problems in relationships.

During stressful times, it is more difficult to express feelings or needs.

Partners react in a conflict with destructive behaviors.

Character, timing, and stress are three major factors that significantly influence communication.

Some people find it difficult to express their feelings verbally.

One-third of participants feel they need assistance to improve communication.

Insight

Approach

Most people felt a positive emotion after an argument. They felt relieved and clear, and sometimes even hopeful, which means that arguing can have positive effects. However, they also reported feelings such as “sadness, disappointment, confusion, and hurt,” indicating that communication during arguments can be destructive.

This is where we wanted to intervene and try to create support that can dissolve these destructive patterns.

Additionally, it became clear that many couples simply lack the time to engage with or improve their communication.

Our HMW

How can we give couples with little time the space to improve their communication, develop their relationship further, and strengthen their connection?

Bio

Paula is happy with the relationship but wishes that she and Tim could improve their communication. Since becoming parents, their communication has become very functional; it lacks warmth, and she can’t always fully understand Tim—even though they have known each other for a long time. Generally, Paula can express her feelings and needs well, but sometimes she notices that Tim feels hurt by her words. This occasionally leads to arguments, leaving both confused and disappointed. Paula often realizes afterward that she wanted to say this or that, but during the argument, her mind wasn’t clear enough. This frustrates her.

Motivation

  • Improve communication
  • More time for communication and relationship development
  • Wants to know how to communicate without hurting Tim
  • Gain clarity about her feelings and thoughts

Frustration

  • Little time and space for communication
  • Misunderstandings
  • Too impulsive during arguments, finds it hard to think clearly

Graphic Elements

Icons

Colors
As primary colors, we use the neutral shades of light gray and black. Our accent color is a vibrant, modern green, which creates a beautiful contrast with the neutral colors. Green symbolizes harmony, balance, and growth. It can create a calming and peaceful atmosphere that is conducive to positive and healthy communication.

Grid
Format: iPhone 16 – 390 × 488 px, Columns: 4, Margin: 24, Gutter: 8

Typography
The TT Neoris is a simple and comfortable neo-grotesque typeface with neutral characteristics, while also appearing very contemporary. Its appearance is technological, which forms a nice contrast to the organic and rounded shapes that characterize the design of our app. It is exceptionally functional and versatile – offering 21 font styles, making it adaptable enough that we do not need any additional fonts in our design.

Graphic Elements
Instead of a world of images, we use circular shapes and speech bubbles filled with various gradients in our app. The gradients embody the feelings of the users. Through these graphic elements, we add more color to our design and also a playful component.

Learnings

High fidelity wireframes simplify the design process. Our wireframes are more conceptual and less visually oriented, which made it take a while to build the prototype.

The first problem we encountered was finding inspirations that could help us with our concept. Unfortunately, there are very few or almost no options when searching for apps for couples that can help improve their communication and relationship.

Another issue was that we chose two types of concepts — bentobox and neobrutalism — and tried to combine them to develop our app, but it took a long time to reach the final point because we didn’t want the app’s design and its intuitiveness to be difficult for the user.
